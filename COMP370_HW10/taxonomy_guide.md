1. Motivation & Context
    Universities host diverse communities. The discussions among this diverse communities span academics, socials, facilities, etc. Reddit forums are a great place to observe these unfiltered discussions. A typology helps us classify the content into coherent categories. 

2. Overview of Types & Their Relationships
a) Exams & Courses
b) Academic Admin / Degree Progress
c) Campus Facilities & Services
d) Social Activities & Clubs
e) Jobs & Opportunities
f) Health, Immigration & Well-Being
g) Governance & Student Politics
h) General Discussion 

Academic categories: a & b share a common theme of education
General Discussion: Posts lacking clear context.

3. List of Types with Definitions, Examples, and Rationale
a. Exams & Courses
    Definition:
    Course-related inquiries

    Positive examples:
    Acco 330 Final Mock Exams -> referances exam
    Mandatory labs ? (Comm 213) -> question about course 

    Negative examples:
    CENGAGE SUCKS -> about a platform, not about a course
   
    Edge Cases:
    Tutoring requests -> Job Opportunity

b. Academic Admin / Degree Progress
    Definition:
    Administrative processes: grades, transcripts, registration, eligibility, graduation, residency classification.

    Positive examples:
    Prof inputted wrong grade on my courses → grading correction
    PDF of your degree → degree documentation request

    Negative examples:
    econ230d1 midterm → academic content → Exams & Courses

    Edge cases:
    Residency classification for tuition → stays here, not Health/Immigration

c. Campus Facilities & Services

    Definition:
    Physical infrastructure or services: printing, gyms, locker rooms, navigation, food services, safety issues.

    Positive examples:
    Where do people print stickers around here? → printing service
    PSA: Theft at Gym Lockers → campus safety

    Negative examples:
    Tier 2 open vball! → social sports → Social Activities

    Edge cases:
    SSMU Free Lunches → treat as service, not student politics

d. Social Activities & Clubs
    Definition:
    Student clubs, sports, events, meetups, recreational activities.

    Positive examples:
    McGill Speedcube Club is here! → club announcement
    Anyone wants to play ARC Raiders?” → social gaming

    Negative examples:
    Gym at McGill → facility, not a social event

    Edge cases:
    Giveaways (e.g., chemistry kit) → classify as community/social

e. Jobs & Opportunities
    Definition:
    Paid studies, tutoring jobs, job requests, hiring, internships.

    Positive examples:
    COMP 233 Tutoring Needed!! → paid work
    Does anyone know about a job? → job search

    Negative examples:
    Phil200 pls help → course help → Exams & Courses

    Edge cases:
    Paid research studies → count as Jobs if payment-focused

f. Health, Immigration & Well-Being
    Definition:
    Healthcare access, insurance, mental health, immigration paperwork, well-being struggles.

    Positive examples:
    Greenshield health insurance access → health insurance
    Replacement of an Immigration Document → immigration admin

    Negative examples:
    McGill won’t process my Quebec residency → Academic Admin

    Edge cases:
    Research study participation → if framed as health support → here; if framed as paid → Jobs

g. Governance & Student Politics

    Definition:
    Student government, CSU/SSMU elections, political discussions tied to campus governance.

    Positive examples:
    PSA: Vote in CSU By-Elections → student politics
    How do I vote in the CSU elections? → governance action

    Negative examples:
    life if I never got another SSMU email again → rant → General

    Edge cases:
    Political memes → only include if campus-related

h. General Discussion / Rants / Misc
    Definition:
    Opinions, rants, vague questions, memes, or posts not fitting any other category.

    Positive examples:
    Leacock is hell → rant
    Why do people uptalk? → general behavior discussion

    Negative examples:
    Comp-206 midterm postponed? → clear course content

    Edge cases:
    Extremely vague posts (“What should I do?”) → default here

4. Argument for Comprehensiveness
The typology was developed through the open coding of 100 reddit posts(50 from mcgill, 50 from concordia)
Every post fall under a category, we cover the whole data
